title: Piker Score
description: How the composite ranking score works
---

The Piker Score is the single number that determines your Pro Mode rank and tier. It's a composite of five performance dimensions, weighted to reward consistent and risk-managed trading over one-off lucky wins.

## Score components

| Component | Weight | What it measures |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| Return on Investment (ROI) | 30% | Raw performance — did you make money? |
| Risk-Adjusted Return (Sharpe) | 25% | Penalises lottery strategies that win big occasionally but lose consistently |
| Maximum Drawdown | 20% | Rewards capital preservation — a trader who never loses 30% of their account outranks one who lost 50% and recovered |
| Consistency Score | 15% | Measures equity curve smoothness — steady compounders rank higher than one-trade wonders |
| Activity | 10% | Minimum participation threshold — prevents gaming the system with a single lucky trade |

## Why this scoring system

A pure ROI leaderboard rewards gamblers. The trader who opens a 100x position and gets lucky once tops the board, even if they blow up every other week.

The Piker Score makes that strategy unviable. High drawdown crushes your score. Low consistency crushes your score. Low activity keeps you off the board entirely.

<Info>
  The scoring system is designed so that the best real-world traders — disciplined, consistent, risk-aware — rank highest. Not the luckiest.
</Info>

## Score visibility

Your Piker Score is always visible in your Pro Dashboard and on your public profile. The five component bars show where your score is strongest and where you have room to improve. This breakdown is public — backers can see exactly which dimensions you're strongest in before deciding to fund you.

## How your score changes

Your score updates continuously as you trade. Opening a large position can temporarily affect your Sharpe ratio — the trade view shows a warning when your current order size is likely to impact your score. Closing positions, taking profits, and maintaining a smooth equity curve all improve your score over time.

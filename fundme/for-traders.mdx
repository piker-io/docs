## Eligibility requirements

To enable Fund Me, you must meet all of the following:

<Steps>
  <Step title="14+ days traded in Rookie Mode">
    You need at least 14 days of Rookie Mode trading history. This gives backers enough data to evaluate your consistency.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Top 20% on Rookie leaderboard">
    Your all-time Rookie ranking must place you in the top 20% of all Rookie traders. This filters for genuine skill.
  </Step>
  <Step title="60%+ profitable days in Rookie">
    More than 60% of your Rookie trading days must be profitable. This is the most important signal for backers — consistency beats a few big wins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Active Pro Mode account">
    You must have Pro Mode active and have placed at least one Pro trade.
  </Step>
</Steps>

When all four criteria are met, a **Fund Me eligibility card** appears on your Rookie Profile with a green tick against each requirement and an "Enable Fund Me" button.

## Setting up Fund Me

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set your backing cap">
    Choose the maximum total USDC you're willing to accept from backers — between $100 and $10,000. This protects you from taking on more responsibility than you want.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your pitch">
    140 characters to tell backers why they should fund you. Your Rookie stats speak for themselves — use the pitch to add personality or describe your trading approach.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go live">
    Once live, your profile shows a Fund Me card visible to any Piker user. You also appear with a green Fund Me indicator on the Pro leaderboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing your backers

Your Fund Me dashboard shows all active backers, how much each has deployed, and their earnings week to date. You can pause Fund Me at any time — existing backers remain until their withdrawal window opens, but no new backers can join while paused.

<Warning>
  Taking on backer capital is a responsibility. Backers trust you with real USDC. Your trading decisions affect their money. The 80/15/5 split reflects that responsibility — you get the majority, but you owe consistent performance.
</Warning>

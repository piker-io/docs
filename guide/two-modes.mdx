Piker has two completely distinct modes. They look different, feel different, and serve different purposes. Understanding the difference is the most important thing to know before you start.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Rookie Mode">
    **The practice arena. Free forever.**

    Every day you receive $100 of virtual capital. Trade it however you want. At 23:59 UTC it resets — your positions close, your capital reloads, and the leaderboard starts fresh.

    - No fees, no evaluations, no gates
    - Daily $100 capital, resets every night at 23:59 UTC
    - Ranked by net P&L on the $100 each day
    - Streaks, badges, daily challenges
    - Full chart, order book, all 20 pairs available
    - Screenshots show a silver **ROOKIE** watermark

    Rookie Mode is a complete product in itself — not just a tutorial. Thousands of traders compete daily for the top spot on the $100 leaderboard.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Pro Mode">
    **The real arena. Real stakes.**

    Pro Mode is ongoing — no daily reset, no seasons. You trade with real or community-backed capital. Your performance is tracked continuously and converted into a Piker Score that determines your tier and vault eligibility.

    - Real capital (your own or community-funded via Fund Me)
    - Continuous leaderboard ranked by Piker Score
    - Five tiers: Bronze, Silver, Gold, Diamond, Elite
    - Fund Me unlocks at consistent top performance
    - On-chain rank verification via Solana
    - Screenshots show a gold **PRO** watermark

    Pro Mode is where reputation is built and real money is earned.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## How the two modes relate

<Info>
  Both modes are always accessible. Switching between them is instant — tap the mode pill in the top left of any screen. Your Rookie stats and Pro stats are tracked separately and displayed separately on your profile.
</Info>

The modes are designed to feed each other. Strong Rookie performers unlock Fund Me eligibility in Pro Mode. Your Rookie streak history appears on your public profile alongside your Pro Piker Score. The two worlds are visually distinct — silver/purple for Rookie, gold/purple for Pro — so anyone who sees a screenshot knows instantly which mode it came from.
